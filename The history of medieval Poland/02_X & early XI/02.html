The first ruler and the first king of Poland

Mieszko I

The Polish history begins here, in 996 the first known ruler of Poland, Duke Mieszko I, converted to Christianity.
He also was the first ruler from the Piast dynasty, which would rain over these land for another four centauris.

Bolesław the Brave

Bolesław I the Brave was a great leader and he expanded the kingdom greatly. He tried to spread Christianity to the neighbouring land, but he suffered a setback when his greatest missionary, Adalbert of Prague, was killed in Prussia.
In the year 1000, The Holy Roman Emperor Otto III recognized the Archbishopric of Gniezno, an institution crucial for the continuing existence of the sovereign Polish state.
In 1025 year Mieszkos' son became a true king, and Poland became a Kingdome.
