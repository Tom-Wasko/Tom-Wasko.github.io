Casimir IV Jagiellon

The succeesor of Władysław II was Władysław III, who soon died in battle against the Ottoman Empire.
After three years Władysław’s brother, Casimir IV Jagiellon, took over the throne in 1447.

Douring his time as king, he expanded Polish borders to Royal Prussia, he also thought the Thirteen Years' War of 1454–66 with the Teutonic state. In 1666 The Peace of Thorn was signed, this treaty divided Prussia to create East Prussia, which was still govern by the knigts, but it was a vassal to Poland.

Poland also confronted the Ottoman Empire and the Crimean Tatars in the south, and helped Lithuania fight the Grand Duchy of Moscow in the east.

The country was developing as a feudal state, which results in the growing influence of Szlachta.
