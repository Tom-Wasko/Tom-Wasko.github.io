The restoration of poland

In 1295 Przemysław II was the first ruler since Bolesław II to be crowned as king of Poland, but his kingship was short lived, becouse he was soon assassinated. His sucessor was Wenceslaus II of Bohemia also reigned as king of Poland.
In 1308, the Teutonic Knights seized Gdańsk and the surrounding region of Pomerelia.
The Piast Kingdom was effectively restored under Władysław I the Elbow-high (1306–33)

King Casimir III the Great (1333–70), Władysław's son was the last of the Piast dynasty. Under his rule the kingdom was strengthened and expanded, but the western provinces of Silesia and most of Polish Pomerania were lost for centuries to come.

In 1364 the Congress of Kraków was formed, it was an anti-turkish crusade. In the same year the Jagiellonian University was founded, it’s to this day one of the best universities in Europe.

The end of The Piast dynsty

The Piast royal line died out in 1370, after the death of Casimir III, Louis I of Hungary took over the throne. In 1384 His daughter Jadwiga assumed the Polish throne.
