Jadwiga’s and Władysław’s rule

In 1386 Grand Duke Jogaila of Lithuania converted to Catholicism and married Queen Jadwiga, then he changed his name to Władysław II Jagiełło. Their marriage brought the Polish–Lithuanian union. It was one of the biggest political entities in Europe for the next four centuries. Queen Jadwiga died in 1399, and the kingdom fell under the rule of her husband.

In 1410 Battle of Grunwald took place, it’s one of the biggest battles in polish history. It was a great victory for the poles and Lithuanians against the Teutonic Knights, but they weren’t able to follow it with a device blow on the Malbork Castle.

The Union of Horodło in 1413 made the bond between Poland and Lithuania even stronger.

In 1425 the rule of Neminem captivabimus protected the Szlachta from being deteined, this and many more laws made the privliges of the polish szlahta even bigger.
