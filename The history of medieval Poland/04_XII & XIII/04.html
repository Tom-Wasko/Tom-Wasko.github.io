The testament of Bolesław III Wrymouth

Bolesław III divided Poland among his sons, this resulted in the slow erodion of the Piast dynasty:

  • In 1180 Casmir II the Just granted immunities and additional privileges to the Polish Church at the Congress of Łęczyca for the title of senior duke.
  • In 1226 Konrad I of Masovia invited the Teutonic Knights to help him fight the Baltic Prussian pagans. The knights defeted the Prussians, but kept their land, which resulted in centuries of conflicts.
  • In 1240 the first Mongol invasion of Poland began, the Mongols desimatet Polish troups i the battle of Legnica in 1421, but luckily they needed to retreat becouse of internal affers.
  • As the period of fragmentation brought economic development and growth of towns. New cities were founded and existing settlements were granted town status per Magdeburg Law.
  • In 1264, Bolesław the Pious granted Jewish liberties in the Statute of Kalisz.
